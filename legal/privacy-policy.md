# Privacy Policy

**Last updated: 18 June 2026**

This Privacy Policy explains how **Octogle Technologies CO L.L.C**, a company incorporated in Dubai, United Arab Emirates ("**Octogle**", "**Leadey**", "**we**", "**us**", or "**our**"), collects, uses, discloses, and protects personal data in connection with the Leadey platform, websites, applications, APIs, and related services (collectively, the "**Service**").

We are committed to handling personal data in accordance with the **UAE Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 on the Protection of Personal Data (PDPL)** and, where applicable, other data-protection laws including the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation ("**GDPR**").

This Privacy Policy should be read together with our Terms and Conditions.

---

## 1. Our Role: Controller and Processor

Leadey is a business-to-business lead-generation and sales-engagement platform. Our role in relation to personal data depends on the context:

- **As a controller**, we determine the purposes and means of processing for: account registration and administration, billing and payments, platform security, product analytics, and our own marketing and communications.
- **As a processor**, we process personal data contained in **Customer Data** and **Lead Data** (for example, the contact records, leads, notes, and campaign data that our customers upload, discover, or generate) on behalf of and under the instructions of our customers, who are the controllers of that data.

If you are an individual whose data has been uploaded to or processed through the Service by one of our customers, that customer is the controller of your data, and you should direct privacy requests to them in the first instance. We will assist our customers in responding to such requests as required by law.

## 2. Personal Data We Collect

### 2.1 Account and profile data
Name, email address, telephone number, job title, organisation name, authentication identifiers, profile settings, and team-membership information of users who register for and use the Service.

### 2.2 Billing and transaction data
Billing contact details, subscription and Credit purchase history, and limited payment metadata. Card and payment details are handled by our third-party payment processor; we do not store full card numbers.

### 2.3 Usage and device data
Log data, IP address, browser and device information, pages and features accessed, actions taken, timestamps, and diagnostic information collected automatically when you use the Service.

### 2.4 Communications data
Records of your communications with us (such as support requests), and content you create within the Service such as notes, templates, and messages.

### 2.5 Telephony and call data
When you use calling features, we and our telephony provider process call metadata (such as caller and recipient numbers, duration, direction, and disposition) and, where enabled, **call recordings and transcripts**.

### 2.6 Customer Data and Lead Data (processed on behalf of customers)
Personal data relating to third-party business contacts and leads that customers import, discover, enrich, or process through the Service. This may include names, business contact details, job titles, employer information, professional profiles, and engagement history. We process this data as a processor on behalf of our customers.

## 3. How We Use Personal Data

We use personal data to:

- provide, operate, maintain, and secure the Service;
- create and administer accounts and authenticate users;
- process payments, Credits, and billing;
- enable platform features such as discovery, enrichment, outreach campaigns, telephony, recording, and transcription, as instructed by customers;
- provide customer support and respond to enquiries;
- monitor, analyse, and improve the performance, reliability, and usability of the Service;
- detect, prevent, and address fraud, abuse, security incidents, and violations of our Terms;
- comply with legal obligations and enforce our agreements; and
- send service-related and, where permitted, marketing communications (you may opt out of marketing at any time).

We do not sell personal data.

## 4. Legal Bases for Processing

Where the GDPR or similar laws apply, we rely on the following legal bases:

- **Performance of a contract** — to provide the Service to you and administer your Account.
- **Legitimate interests** — to operate, secure, analyse, and improve the Service, and to conduct B2B marketing, provided these interests are not overridden by your rights.
- **Consent** — where required (for example, certain cookies or marketing); you may withdraw consent at any time.
- **Legal obligation** — to comply with applicable laws.

Where we act as a processor, our customers are responsible for establishing the lawful basis for processing Lead Data.

## 5. Artificial Intelligence Features

Certain features (such as call transcription and summarisation) use artificial-intelligence services. Where these features are used, relevant content (such as call recordings or text) may be processed by our AI sub-processors solely to deliver the feature. We do not permit our AI sub-processors to use Customer Data to train their general models except as necessary to provide the service and as governed by our agreements with them.

## 6. How We Share Personal Data

We share personal data only as described below:

### 6.1 Sub-processors and service providers
We use trusted third parties to help operate the Service, which may include:

- **Hosting and infrastructure** providers;
- **Telephony and messaging** providers (for calling, SMS, and recording);
- **Payment processing** providers;
- **Authentication and identity** providers;
- **Data sourcing and enrichment** providers (for discovery and enrichment features);
- **Artificial-intelligence** providers (for transcription and summarisation);
- **Analytics, logging, and email** providers.

These providers are authorised to process personal data only as necessary to perform services for us and are bound by appropriate confidentiality and data-protection obligations.

### 6.2 Between customer users
Personal data within a customer Account (such as activity, notes, and assignments) may be visible to other Authorised Users within the same organisation.

### 6.3 Legal and protection
We may disclose personal data where required by law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request, or where necessary to protect the rights, property, or safety of Octogle, our users, or others, and to enforce our Terms.

### 6.4 Business transfers
If we are involved in a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, personal data may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to this Privacy Policy.

## 7. International Data Transfers

We are based in the United Arab Emirates and may process and store personal data in the UAE and in other countries where we or our sub-processors operate. Where personal data is transferred across borders, we take steps to ensure an appropriate level of protection in accordance with applicable law, which may include standard contractual clauses or other lawful transfer mechanisms.

## 8. Data Retention

We retain personal data for as long as necessary to provide the Service, fulfil the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements.

- Account and billing data is generally retained for the life of the Account and for a reasonable period thereafter as required by law.
- Customer Data and Lead Data are retained according to the customer's instructions and configuration; we will delete or return such data after termination in accordance with our Terms, subject to legal retention requirements.
- Call recordings and transcripts are retained according to customer configuration and applicable law.

## 9. Security

We implement reasonable technical and organisational measures designed to protect personal data against unauthorised access, loss, misuse, or alteration, including access controls, encryption in transit, and authentication safeguards. No system is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. You are responsible for keeping your account credentials confidential.

## 10. Your Rights

Subject to applicable law (including the UAE PDPL and, where applicable, the GDPR), you may have the right to:

- **access** the personal data we hold about you;
- request **correction** of inaccurate or incomplete data;
- request **erasure** of your data;
- **restrict** or **object** to certain processing;
- request **portability** of data you provided to us;
- **withdraw consent** where processing is based on consent; and
- **lodge a complaint** with the competent data-protection authority (in the UAE, the UAE Data Office).

To exercise these rights with respect to data for which we are the controller, contact us using the details in Section 14. If your data was uploaded by one of our customers (who is the controller), please contact that customer directly; we will support them as required.

We will respond to requests within the timeframe required by applicable law. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling a request.

## 11. Call Recording and Monitoring

Where call recording and transcription features are enabled by a customer, the customer is responsible for providing all legally required notices and obtaining all legally required consents from call participants. Recording-consent requirements vary by jurisdiction; some require the consent of all parties.
